Five Notable Computer/Gaming Tech Milestones

The 1st Milestone:Pong:1972

The Creation of Pong: California entrepreneur Nolan Bushnell hires young engineer Al Alcorn to design a car-driving game, but since this is 1972, this was too ambitious for the time, he instead has Alcorn design a version of Ping Pong. The game was tested in bars in Grass Valley and Sunnyvale, California, where it proved very popular. Pong would revolutionize the arcade industry and launch the modern video game era.After the invention of Pong, the world of computer technology would forever change in the application and use of computer technology in the world of gaming.




The 2nd Milestone:The Atari 2600:1977

Atari releases its Video Computer System (VCS) later renamed the Atari 2600. The VCS was the first widely successful video game system, selling more than twenty million units throughout the 1980s. The VCS used the 8-bit MOS 6507 microprocessor and was designed to be connected to a home television set. When the last of Atari’s 8-bit game consoles were made in 1990, more than 900 game titles had been released. The Atari 2600 is the first major step into the fresh new console market. Being quite the achievement and bringing more acessiblity playability to video games as during this time, you would have to have a computer to play any type of game, and this console introduced many people for the first time into video games.




The 3rd Milestone:NES:1983

Since 1983, the American electronic game market had been depressed due to a overabundance of poorly performing games and consoles. That same year, Nintendo released its Famicom gaming system in Japan. Renamed the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) when it was released in North America, the NES started to turn the tables of the fortunes of the American game industry. The system launched with eighteen available titles, and was largely responsible for turning Mario the Plumber into one of the most enduring characters in the history of video games.The NES is also one of those consoles that carries a certain nostagia to the people, especially the children of that time, due to how important it is the gaming as a whole and many people remember and recognize the NES over the Atari 2600 because of it being better as a whole. A sure advancement in gaming technology.




The 4th Milestone:The Gameboy:1990

Handheld electronic games had been popular for more than a decade by the time Nintendo introduces the Game Boy. The system used removable game cartridges to play on its 2.9-inch black and white screen. Game Boy's popularity was helped by its major release title, the puzzle game Tetris. Over nearly twenty years, more than one hundred million Game Boys were sold, making it one of the all-time, top-selling game systems. The Gameboy is, by far, one of the most recognizable gaming systems of all time. This was due to the ability to play video games on the go, mobile. Not only was this system the next important step in gaming tech, it also released with the one of most sold and recognizable games of all time:Tetris. Making the console an instant hit just for Tetris itself.




The 5th Milestone:Doom:1990

An immersive first-person shooter-style game, Doom becomes popular on many different platforms. Initially distributed via USENET newsgroups, Doom attracted a massive following. Doom players were also among the first to customize the game’s levels and appearance though ‘modding.’ Some criticized the level of violence portrayed in Doom, and it was cited as a prime reason for US Congressional hearings on video game violence in 1995. Doom spawned several sequels and a 2005 film. Doom is, like Tetris, an insanely important step in video gaming history, creating a model and design method to be used and improved upon for decades to come. This title also released on computers, mainly. Doom is also important is the fact of its seemingly inspiration of the modding community. A community that is massively grown over time because of its various mods, changing and improving games that can be modded, as well as creating new content. The community has grown so much that most game developers make their games with modding in mind as it increases the lifespan of the game and the communities love of the game.




The 6th Milestone:Playstation, Xbox, and GPUs:1955-2020

The information era is in full swing, with information at our literal and metaphysical fingertips. After the aforementioned developments, more were made progressively , further advancing. Consoles like the Playstation and Xbox got quite the attention with there advancements and have made further installations and new, more high tech consoles to this day, even releasing new competing consoles as recent as 2020, the Playstation 5 and the Xbox Series X. These new advancement in console and computer technology promoted more advancement through pushing their limits through new games and systems, creating and everlasting cycle of progress that has, and will certainly have an affect in the years to come.
